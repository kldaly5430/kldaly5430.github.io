About

My name is Kyle Daly. I am currently studying computer science at Central Michigan University. I am currently the president of the Central Michigan Video Game Development and Design Club. Before going to Central Michigan University I attended Jackson College in Jackson, Michigan. There I received an Associates in Applied Science in Computer Programming.
I been interested in video games for since I was little. That passion for video games hasn't changed. And now instead of just playing games I want to make them. I've always loved playing RPGs. I like games that allow to explore and learn about the world around you. RPGs are the best because they allow you to feel as if you are apart of the world, not just someone is watching the world. Your decisions shape the world. For the past 5 years I have been apart of a Dungeons & Dragons group. Playing through the game has been amazing, so much that I have made it my personal project to try to re-create our Dungeons & Dragons campaign into a video game.
For the past two years at Central Michigan University I have done Global Game Jam, a 24-hackathon, and a 24 Create! event. Each year our club hosts Global Game Jam and in the prior weeks during our club times run tutorials for people new to Unity so that they can participate even if they have had no experience in making video games. This past semester our club hosted Create!. Create! was a 24-hour event that allowed students to create any kind of project that wanted. It gave students a chance to learn something new, form groups to work on projects, or a space to work. during Create! two of my friends and I created an escape/puzzle game.
